Chapter 13: Nickname

After the Coronation, I was asked by the Royal Advisor to meet Queen Hina -- the Oujo-sama -- at the garden. “She wants to tell you something important,” he said.

I nodded, started getting dressed up, and went down to meet her as instructed. From there, Hina was tending to the pots as usual. “How was the coronation?” I asked.

“It was fun,” she said. “I’m actually glad that you were there to watch my reign. Looks like everything was worth it in the end.”

“Yeah,” I nervously chuckle. “Anyway, why did you bring me here?”

“Well, um…”

Hina then turns her back, picking something up from the perch. “Agatha, I might’ve known you for a few days, but every time I look at you… I feel like I need to protect somebody. Somebody who’s willing to put themselves on the line for the sake of someone.”

“Hm?”

She turns back to my direction. “Agatha… or, should I just call you…”

Sighing, she slowly blurts out what would appear to be the nickname that she made just for me.

“Aga-chuu?”

…

“Um?” I tilt my head.

She smiles. “I know your brain’s a bit fuzzy, but I just want to tell you that I’ve been meaning to give you something special as a thanks for always having my back. It’s been at the back of my head since I’m trying to make up nicknames for my subjects, so I figured I should give you something in that regard too.”

“Aga-chuu…” I begin to memorize the nickname she gave to me many times in my head. “That’s… a cute nickname.”

“I know, right?”

We both giggle afterwards. Hearing the nickname over and over again felt like I already had a special place in her heart. The maids were right -- she’s pretty easy once I get used to working with her.

“So, Aga-chuu,” she said, carrying an item. “Will you accept this?”

“What?”

The item she showed me is a pair of wrist beads. “I made these myself.”

Looking at the wrist beads she showed me, there’s a slight tint of pink on otherwise pure-green beads. Even so, there's a number of glitters all over the beads; and the bands holding them in place are comfortable enough to wear on the wrist for long hours.

“These are nice,” I said, taking one of the wrist beads from her hands. Wearing it, the beads emit a soft glow of gradient green and pink that encircles around my wrist.

Hina then slowly inserts the wrist bead into her wrist, also reacting the same way as mine. Some few moments later, she begins to shriek. “Uwaah!”

“Um, are you okay?”

“I’m fine,” she said, shaking her wrist. “That shook me for a second… I feel like I saw a sudden flashback or something.”

“What did you see?”

“It’s…”
